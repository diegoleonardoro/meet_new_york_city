About Meet NYC

Thanks for taking the time to browse this website.

This website has a very strong personal value to me. It is a reflection of some of the most meaningful experiences that I have had in New York City.

I was born in Colombia, and I have the fortune to be living in NYC since 2016. I came to this city for a Masters and my first job was at a non profit organization called Historic Districts Council (HDC).

Over the years, HDC has worked to preserve the architectural heritage of NYC, but they hired me because they wanted to explore an additional focus to their mission: preserving NYCâ€™s cultural heritage of underrepresented communities.

In doing this job, I have known this city in a way that I could have never imagined. I have worked with communities all over The Bronx, from Hunts Points to Fieldstone; I have met with elected officials and community groups from Sunset Park, Bushwick, Jackson Heights, Astoria, Flushing, Elmhurst, and other neighborhoods across the city with substantial immigrant, Hispanic and African American communities.

During this process, I have come to the conclusion that what makes NYC so unique are not the most obvious destinations and associations that people have of this city. It is the cultures, the neighborhoods, the communities that live beyond the skyscrapers and Central Park what make this city so unique.

This is a web-based project that aims to highlight the overlooked neighborhoods of NYC by encouraging residents to write about their favorite places in their neighborhoods, and explain why those places are important to them.

Working at the Historic Districts Council I have realized that all neighborhoods have passionate residents who love the place they live in, who know the history, the best restaurants, destinations and the people who make their neighborhood meaningful not only to them but to the history of the city.

These residents are the right people to show their neighborhood to visitors.

As I mentioned, this web site has a strong personal value to me because it is a reflection of some of the most meaningful experiences that I have had in New York City. One of those experiences is the work with communities that I already explained, and the other one is my journey to become a better professional with skill sets that allow me to build something that can actually have an impact in society.

I majored in journalism and I have a Masters in Strategic Communications. Three years ago I felt the need to complement my communications skills with technical skills. I was motivated by the idea of becoming a data journalist, so I went to a data science bootcamp.

Since 2018, I have been coding and studying to become a better programmer. This website is the result of that process. I coded every single line of this website, and while it is not perfect yet, it shows how my idea of highlighting communities could eventually look like.

If I win the David Prize, I will be able to partner with more programmers and web designers to make this platform look as beautiful as you could imagine.
